D.O. Penedès

The region is truly one of the most popular and ancient viticultural areas in Europe, dotted with quaint villages, picturesque churches and vineyards upon vineyards growing a wide selection of grape varieties including Macabeo, Paradella, Xarello, Chardonnay and Pinot Noir.

Penedès is a privileged territory for winemaking and there are a bunch of reasons for that. The variety of the soils, differences in climate, and land result in a thousand aromas, bodies and structures.

Location

Penedès is a wide and open region located southwest of Barcelona. The wine region lies between two coastal provinces — Barcelona, in the north, and Tarragona further south.

Being settled between the blue Mediterranean and the Garraf Massif, geographically, Penedés is split into three distinct zones: The Penedès Superior, Marítim, and Central.

climate

The region can boast its diversity of wine styles thanks to the Mediterranean climate with warm summers, mild winters, and moderate rainfall concentrated mostly in spring and autumn.

What makes the region unique is its differing micro-climates. The proximity to the sea and the different altitudes of each area allow winemakers to generate a relatively wide range of wine styles.

cellar

The region is home to hundreds of wineries. Our winery is located in the heart of Penedès Superior. To experience the best winemaking traditions and soak up the atmosphere of the region we invite you to savour wines from our cellars.

wine

NuThe wineries of Penedès have been producing fine wines for centuries. The region enjoys a wide variety of wines including dry reds and whites, sparkling whites and roses made using both the Traditional Method and Champagne Method also known as Cava.

Diversity is the word that characterises Penedès best. The principal white grapes include Macabeo, Xarel-lo, Parellada, Subirat-Parent and Chardonnay, the reds are Tempranillo, Cabernet Sauvignon, Garnacha Tinta and Cariñena.

champagne

Although Penedès covers all types of wines styles its primary product is Cava, sparkling wine made in the Champagne Method. More than 80% of Cava cellars are located within the DO.

Local cava is made with the Catalan grapes Xarel-lo and Parellada and often blended with Chardonnay and Macabeo. An iconic fine sparkling wine of the Mediterranean region has a unique spectrum of aromas and flavours.

Priorat

Priorat is one of the two wine regions of Spain that has been awarded the highest wine qualification, DOQ, by the Government. The main grapes in this area are Grenache, Carignane, Grenache Blanc and a few other varieties. They are characterized by an intense cherry color, with complex aromas of ripe fruit and terroir.

This small and dynamic mountainous region is known for its rocky land, beautiful views and centuries-old vineyards cultivated on terraces. Due to its challenging landscape, local wines are gaining enormous popularity.

Ribera del Duero

One of Spain’s top red wine regions, Ribera del Duero is located about two hours north of Madrid. The wine region in Castilla y Leon produces one of the most complex, flavorful and full-bodied red wines in Spain.

The vast majority of Ribera del Duero wine is made mainly from Tempranillo grapes, over 95% of the vineyards are planted with these grapes thick-skinned black grapes, ripening early to survive short growing seasons. It is capable to produce complex wines that develop over a long period.

Rioja

The region which takes its name after the Oja river is situated on a plateau south of the Cantabrian Mountains. Rioja is one of Spain's leading wine regions, predominantly for Tempranillo grapes known for its fine tannins and outstanding aging capacity.

This world-renowned wine region mainly presents berry-scented, barrel-aged red wines made from Tempranillo and Garnacha. The wine is known for its aromas of strawberry, cherry, and tanned leather when aged.

Navarra

It is one of the most diverse and ancient wine regions in Spain. The story began in the 2nd century BC when the Roman planted the first vines. Later on the outbreak of phylloxera at the end of the 19th century killed off 90% of the region’s vines. Today Navara is gaining its popularity around the wine world producing characterful, terroir-driven wines at highly competitive price points.

Navar’s wines are mainly made from the Tempranillo, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Merlot grapes. An interesting combination of three climates (Atlantic, Continental, and Mediterranean) allows to sample a diverse selection of wines.

Castilla-La Mancha

La Mancha, with its many acres of vineyards, is the largest wine region in the whole of Europe. The region has a long winemaking tradition, as grape growing was practiced there by the Romans and The Moors.

Over the past decades, winemaking has significantly improved. Today the region’s vineyards produce high-quality wine from local grape varieties such as Airen, Macabeo, Tempranillo, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Garnacha.

Albariño, Galicia

The wine region situated in the northwest corner of Spain is surprisingly lush and green. Galicia’s cool, maritime climate makes it more hospitable to white wines.

Albariño is the most important white grape variety in Galicia. Classic notes for Albariño are light-to-medium in body, high in acidity, and very fragrant, with notes of peach, Meyer lemon, and apple blossom.
