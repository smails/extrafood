Passion, honesty and transparency

Barcelona Mediterranean Wine is a winemaking company from sunny Barcelona that has been growing, producing, and distributing confidential Catalan wines over the years. We maintain the highest level of expertise and professionalism to spread the enjoyment of wine. At Barcelona Mediterranean Wine, we are creating uniquely wonderful wine experiences combining both winemaking traditions and innovations.

To guarantee absolute quality in the bottle we use both true traditional and machinery techniques. The winemaking process is done within the highest world standards and is guided by our talented winemakers every step along the way. We believe that our attention to detail in pruning, picking, and winemaking ensures that our Mediterranean Wines will always show great brightness, purity, and quality.

While our hands are working in vineyards, our minds are looking to the future of winemaking. Driven by an entrepreneurial spirit, we embrace innovation and exercise courageous ideas to help shape new tendencies in winemaking. Being deeply involved in the process, we also aim to support and transmit the beauty of the regions where our wines are made.

7 regions, a thousand wines

Our grapes come from the country's most famous wine regions across Spain. Thanks to the climate and land diversity each region has its own grape varieties and wine styles, from the Albariño white wines of Galicia in the northwest, to the Tempranillo red wines of Rioja, to the sparkling Cavas of Penedès.

We invite our consumers to devoir the unique wine world full of sensation.

Penedès

Just an hour south of Barcelona the most important viticultural area in Catalonia is located. It is known for its sparkling Cava wines as well as fine dry reds and whites. The region can boast its diversity of wine styles thanks to the Mediterranean climate and the fertile soils. The region can boast its diversity of wine styles thanks to the Mediterranean climate and the fertile soils.

Navarra

One of Spain's most diverse and ancient wine regions attracts attention for its high-quality red wines made mainly from the Tempranillo, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Merlot grape varieties.

There is an interesting combination of three climates in Navarra (Atlantic, Continental, and Mediterranean), which allows producing a variety of styles of wines.

Priorat

This small and dynamic mountainous wine region is known for its rocky land, beautiful views and centuries-old vineyards cultivated on terraces. Due to its challenging landscape, local wines are gaining enormous popularity.

The dominant vineyard plantings in Priorat are Garnacha and Cariñena. They are characterized by an intense cherry color, with complex aromas of ripe fruit and terroir.

Ribera del Duero

An important wine region in Castilla y Leon, northern Spain produces one of the most complex and flavorful red wines in Spain. The high-quality red wine is made mainly from Tempranillo grapes. The best examples are renowned throughout the wine world.

Rioja

This world-renowned wine region mainly presents berry-scented, barrel-aged red wines made from Tempranillo and Garnacha. The wine is known for its structure, tannins, and a fruity characteristic.

Galicia

The wine region situated in the northwest corner of Spain is surprisingly lush and green. Galicia’s cool, maritime climate makes it more hospitable to white wines.

The most common grape varieties include Albarino, Godello, and Mencia. The region offers fine, often exotically scented, crisp white wines.

Castilla La Mancha

La Mancha, with its many acres of vineyards, is the largest wine region in the whole of Europe. The region has a long winemaking tradition, as grape growing was practiced there by the Romans and The Moors.

Over the past decades, winemaking has significantly improved. Today the region’s vineyards produce high-quality wine from local grape varieties such as Airen, Macabeo, Tempranillo, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Garnacha.
